SECTION 01

    
THE SERVER IS THE GRAVITY WELL

    

      Clients orbit with their own local SQLite — reads and writes stay local and
      instant. Pushes fall toward the commit log, the accretion disk where every
      change is ordered and validated before it crosses the event horizon. Drift
      out of contact and the outbox holds your writes; re-enter range and they
      replay, in order, against the same log everyone else sees.

SECTION 02

    
CAPABILITIES, TYPEWRITTEN

    

      
* scopes-as-code — authorization is part of the schema, enforced at the horizon

      
* offline outbox — writes queue while out of range, replay on reconnect

      
* honest conflicts — surfaced to you, never silently merged

      
* realtime — change propagation over WebSocket

      
* CRDT columns — merge-friendly fields where you want them

      
* blobs — binary payloads ride the same protocol

      
* optional E2EE — the well doesn't need to read your matter

      
* windowed sync — pull only the slice of the log you can see
